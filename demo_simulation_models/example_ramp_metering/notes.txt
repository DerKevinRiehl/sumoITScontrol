Different types of on-ramps
	Version 1: single lane, and has an extra merge lane on the highway for several meters
	Version 2: single lane, no extra merge lane on the highway, needs to merge directly
	Version 3: multiple lanes, with extra merge lane on the highway
	
Implementation Details:

	Simulation:
		Start Simulation earlier than intended for warmup period, so that when you count / measure there is already traffic on the highway

	Network:
		Connection Mode: check conncetions make sense for merging
		Intersection / Junction type: unregulated (visible through white crossing connections rather than some gray)
		Mainline: forbid ChangeRight for the mainline lanes intersecting with extra merge lane
					(visible through orange line)
		
		Signals: on-ramp split edge for extra intersection with traffic light
		Sensors: 
			Ramp Sensors: laneAreaDetector (E2) for queue length (e.g. 50m long) in front of traffic light, 
			Mainline Sensors (never on merging lane(s)): 
				on each lane, can be upstream (e.g. 10 meters), donwstream (e.g. 100m), or in merging area (e.g. in middle)
				can be inductionLoop (E1) or laneAreaDetector (E2)

	Demand:
		Define Routes
		Define 5 types of driving behaviors for each vehicle (e.g. car and motorcycle) type and origin (mainline more cooperative, ramp more aggressive)
		When defining flows, split flows equally to 5 different aggressivity types per vehicle type (e.g. car and motorcycle)
		vTypeDistribution to define probabilities
		
		spawn flow with probability (is geometrically distributed spawning rather than equally distributed) instead of vehsperhour
			(probability is per second, so probability=1.0 equals 3600 veh/h)
		important: select departSpeed="max" and departLane="free" departPos="last" to avoid that vehicles are not properly spawned and congestion happens at begin of network
		important2: even though you spawn a certain amount, it makes sense to double check through measurements how high the flow actually was, as number of vehicles spawned is limited in SUMO
